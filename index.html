CAT FLUTD

What is FLUTD?

FLUTD is the abbreviation of Feline Lower Urinary Tract Disease, which is a general term for a variety of conditions affecting the bladder and urethra. It includes:
  • Urinary calculi
  • Bladder stones
  • Urinary tract infection (UTI)
  • Urinary obstruction
  • Feline idiopathis cystitis (FIC)
It is a critical or even fatal disease if not treated timely. If you are a new cat owner, you might hardly imagine you would need to know about this and take preventive action to fight against FLUTD. It is actually a very common disease for a cat, taking up around 10% of the vet visits. Male cats are more vulnerable to this disease due to the nature that their urethra are generally narrower than the female ones.

Symptoms

  • Frequent urination or straining
  • Meowing or howling during urination
  • Abnormal urinating posture
  • Foul-smelling or cloudy urine
  • Frequent licking of the gential area
  • Urinating outside the litter box
  • Urine in blood

Diagnosis

Kidney generates urine and releases to Bladder through Ureter. Urine will be accumulated in Bladder to the point that the cat feels like urinating. And then urine will go through Urethra to Penis and comes out.

If your feline friend shows symptoms of FLUTD, please visit your primary vet for physical examination. FLUTD contains a broad group of diseases requiring different treatment methods, the following tests are mandatory to help determine the cause of FLUTD:

  • Urinalysis
    • Check if there is bacteria in the urine. If there is, bacteria infection will be the cause.
    • Check if there is crystal in the urine. If there is, crystal will be the cause.
  • Blood Test
    • RBC (Red Blood Cell) - High level indicates disease or disorder
    • WBC (White Blood Cell) - High level indicates infection.
    • Creatinine - High level indicates kidney problems
    • BUN (Blood Urea Nitrogen) - High level indicates kidney failure or diseases
  • Radiograph
    • Check if there is stone in the bladder. If there is, stone will the cause.
  • FIC - Feline Idiopathis Cystitis
    • If the vet couldn't find bacteria, crystal or stone through the above tests, it will be diagnosed as FIC. FIC can be mild but also can be critical. When your feline friend cannot urinate, it is having urethra obstruction, which will cause acute kidney failure consecutively.

Treatment

  • Relief of blockage

    • If the urinary passage is blocked, relief is absolutely essential. This usually requires anesthesia (unless the cat is already is in a coma). Although anaesthesia is dangerous because of the cat's poor condition, there is no alternative. Most of these blockages yield to flushing by catherisation (passing a fine tube) up the urethra. In case where the 'stones' are unusually stubborn abdominal puncture of the bladder may be necessary. The catheter may be left in place for a few days and a restraint collar put on the cat to prevent him removing the catheter. The chances of success are directly proportional to how quickly the cat has been brought in for treatment.

  • Fluid therapy

    • This is indicated when the cat is dehydrated or toxic. This consists of intravenous administration of large volumes of balanced fluids.

  • Antibiotics

    • Used to take care of infections that may develop as a result of abnormalities in the urinary tract

Care

  • Food & Water

    • Prescription wet food is strongly recommended. Hill's, Purina, Royal Canin and Blue Buffalo all carry urinary diet food. Wet food typically contains at least 70% of water, providing your feline friend surviving water input if it doesn't like to drink water.

    Water fountain may be helpful in attracting your cat to drink more water. Broth is also an option to encourage drinking but needs to be kept fresh.

  • Litter Box

    • Litter box needs to be cleaned as frequent as you can, ideally once a day. It is advised to monitor the shape of urine clump:

    • Big circle - 3~4 per day indicates a healthy cat
    • Crescent - Cat doesn't like litter in general or this brand of litter specifically
    • Thin cookie - The litter should at least more than 3 inch
    • Small drop - Indication of potential FLUTD

    It is also suggested to maintain n+1 litter box if within financial affordability.

  • Stress Control

    • Feliway is a product that comforts and reassures cats and kittens and reduces signs of stress such as urine spraying, scratching, fighting or hiding. It can be purchased in a spray bottle or as a plug-in diffuser. Feliway for cats works by emulating the natural pheromones released by a mother cat to calm her kittens.

    Petting and time of accompany also matter. Rotating different sets of toys for your cat if possible.
